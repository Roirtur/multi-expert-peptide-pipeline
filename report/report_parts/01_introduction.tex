\section{Introduction}\label{sec:introduction}

Peptides, and especially antimicrobial peptides (AMPs), are promising candidates for therapeutic, agricultural, and industrial applications \cite{wang2022machine}. Their \textit{in silico} design is naturally framed as an optimization loop: generate candidate sequences, apply feasibility constraints, and rank candidates with proxy signals for expected function and risk \cite{wang2022machine,veltri2018deep}. The central difficulty is multi-objective control: improving one criterion (for example activity proxy) can degrade others (such as developability).

This report presents a multi-expert agentic proof of concept that addresses this difficulty through explicit role decomposition. The pipeline combines three specialized agents and one orchestrator: a generator that proposes candidates, a chemistry module that evaluates physicochemical admissibility, and a biology module that provides an embedding-based ranking signal. The orchestrator enforces an iterative cycle (propose $\rightarrow$ filter $\rightarrow$ score $\rightarrow$ revise), aggregates expert feedback, and maintains traceable run logs.

Our objective is an AI-systems evaluation rather than biological validation. We therefore focus on reproducible proxy-based metrics: validity, Top-$K$ quality, diversity, constraint pass rate, and score evolution across rounds, followed by comparison with a non-agentic baseline. The guiding question is whether iterative multi-expert coordination yields measurable gains over one-shot conditional generation.

The remainder of the report is organized as follows. Section \ref{sec:context} positions the work in the recent literature and project scope. Section \ref{sec:architecture} details the pipeline design and agent responsibilities. Section \ref{sec:setup} describes scoring, metrics, and baseline protocol. Section \ref{sec:results} reports empirical behavior and baseline comparison. Section \ref{sec:discussion} discusses limits and extensions, and Section \ref{sec:conclusion} closes the report.
