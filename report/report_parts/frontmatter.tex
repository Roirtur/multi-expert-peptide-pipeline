\title{A Multi-Expert Agentic Pipeline for Peptide Generation}

\author{Fitger~Nathan,~Iliescu~Eduard,~Macdonald~Arthur,~Niels~Roudeau%
\thanks{Universit\'e de Bordeaux, Master 2, 2026.\\
Akka Zemmari, Enzo Eddebbarh, Johan Samot}}

\IEEEtitleabstractindextext{%
\begin{abstract}
We present a proof-of-concept multi-expert agentic pipeline for \textit{in silico} peptide generation \cite{wang2022machine}. A central orchestrator coordinates three specialized agents: an AI Designer that proposes candidates, a Chemistry Constraints agent that enforces proxy feasibility rules (length, net charge, hydrophobicity, and forbidden motifs), and a Biology Function/Risk agent that critiques expected activity and toxicity-related risks \cite{veltri2018deep}. The pipeline iterates through a propose $\rightarrow$ filter $\rightarrow$ score $\rightarrow$ revise loop, with round-level logs for traceability. Evaluation is fully quantitative and reports validity, average and Top-K score, diversity, and constraint pass rates against a non-agentic baseline. Results show that iterative multi-agent coordination improves both constraint satisfaction and top-ranked candidate quality over single-pass generation \cite{wang2022machine,veltri2018deep,rives2021biological}.
\end{abstract}
