\begin{abstract}
We present a proof-of-concept multi-agent pipeline for the iterative \textit{in silico} design of antimicrobial peptides (AMPs).
The system orchestrates three specialised agents --- a \textbf{Generator} (VAE-based designer), a \textbf{Chemist} (physicochemical constraint filtering), and a \textbf{Biologist} (ESM-2 embedding scorer) --- in a closed feedback loop.
Starting from a reference peptide, the pipeline evolves a population of candidates over multiple rounds, retaining top-$K$ sequences at each step.
We describe the architecture, experimental setup, and evaluation metrics, and compare results against a non-agentic random-generation baseline.
\end{abstract}

\subsection{Orchestrator}
The \texttt{BaseOrchestrator} runs for at most $T$ iterations over a population of size $N$, applying validity filtering and activity scoring before selecting the top-$K$ candidates for the next round (Algorithm~\ref{alg:pipeline}).

\begin{algorithm}[H]
\caption{Multi-Expert Peptide Optimisation Loop}
\label{alg:pipeline}
\begin{algorithmic}[1]
\State $P \gets \text{Generator.generate}(N)$
\For{$i = 1$ to $T$}
    \State $P_{\text{valid}} \gets \text{Chemist.filter}(P)$
    \If{$P_{\text{valid}} = \emptyset$} \textbf{break} \EndIf
    \State $S \gets \text{Biologist.score}(P_{\text{valid}})$
    \State $B \gets \text{top-}K(P_{\text{valid}}, S)$
    \State $P \gets B \cup \text{Generator.modify}(B,\, N - K)$
\EndFor
\State \Return $B$
\end{algorithmic}
\end{algorithm}
